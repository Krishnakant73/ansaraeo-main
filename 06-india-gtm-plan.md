# PART 6 of 6 — India Go-To-Market & Distribution Plan

---

## 1. The Core Truth From the Searchable Research (repeated because it's the most important thing in this whole series)

Searchable's 24-hour six-figure launch happened because Chris Donnelly had **2M+ followers built over years** before the product existed. You are very likely starting without that. This isn't discouraging — it's clarifying: **your GTM plan must build the audience and the product in parallel, not sequentially.** Don't wait until the product is "done" to start building distribution.

---

## 2. Distribution Channels, Ranked for an Indian AEO SaaS

### 1. Agency partnerships (fastest path to volume)
- Directly approach 20-30 Indian digital marketing/SEO agencies. Offer them a free white-label pilot for 2-3 of their clients.
- Agencies are financially motivated to offer AEO as a new service line to their existing clients — you're not selling them a tool, you're selling them a new revenue line they can resell.
- One agency deal often converts to 5-30 brands over time as they roll it out across their client base.

### 2. Founder-led content (your own version of Chris Donnelly's playbook, India-focused)
- Pick ONE platform first: **LinkedIn is the best fit for reaching Indian marketing/founder audiences right now** (higher engagement for B2B founder content in India than X/Twitter).
- Content angle: document the build in public — "Day 47 of building India's AI visibility tool," share real data ("We checked 50 Indian D2C brands — only 3 show up when ChatGPT is asked for the best [category] in India"), publish free mini-audits.
- This mirrors exactly what worked for Chris Donnelly — but you're doing it in a market (India, AEO) where nobody has claimed the "the person teaching this" position yet.
- Secondary: short-form video (Instagram Reels/YouTube Shorts) translating the same insights for founders who don't read LinkedIn — Hindi/Hinglish voiceover content on "AI search for your business" is genuinely unclaimed territory in India.

### 3. Free tool as a distribution engine
- The "AI Visibility Checker" from Phase 0 (Part 1) should stay live permanently as a free top-of-funnel tool, like Ahrefs' free tools strategy. People share their results ("We only got mentioned in 2/10 prompts!") — this is organic distribution you don't have to pay for.

### 4. Communities and events
- Indian SEO/marketing communities (Facebook groups, Slack/Discord communities, WhatsApp groups run by agency owners) are highly active and under-served by global tool marketing.
- Sponsor or speak at Indian marketing meetups/conferences (e.g., regional MarTech events, startup ecosystem events) — cheap relative to paid ads, high trust transfer.

### 5. Paid acquisition (later, not first)
- Once you have a proven conversion funnel (Phase 2+), Google/LinkedIn ads targeting "AI SEO," "ChatGPT SEO," "AEO India" keywords — but don't lead with paid spend before you have organic proof points, since CAC will be high and unproven early.

## 3. Pricing Strategy for India

Indian SaaS willingness-to-pay is meaningfully lower than US/UK — price in ₹, not a straight USD-to-INR conversion of Western competitor pricing.

| Tier | Price (indicative) | For |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | ₹1,999–₹2,999/mo | Solopreneurs, small D2C brands, 1 domain, 25-30 prompts, 2-3 engines |
| Growth | ₹6,999–₹9,999/mo | Growing D2C/SME brands, multiple domains, 100+ prompts, all engines, content studio |
| Agency | ₹14,999+/mo | Multi-client agencies, white-label, unlimited client workspaces (tiered by client count) |
| Enterprise | Custom | Larger brands needing dedicated support, custom integrations |

14-day free trial, no credit card required (matches the proven pattern from Searchable's own onboarding, which reviewers specifically liked).

## 4. Messaging Framework

**Headline positioning:** "Is your brand invisible to ChatGPT? See exactly what AI tells your customers about you — in English, Hindi, and the languages your customers actually search in."

**Proof points to lead with once you have them:**
- "We checked X Indian brands in [category] — only Y% show up in AI answers" (this kind of category-level data point, published as a free report, is exactly the tactic Chris Donnelly used to generate leads on LinkedIn — you can do the India-specific version of this immediately, even pre-product, using manual research)
- Specific before/after case studies once you have your first 10-20 paying customers

---

## 5. First 90 Days — Concrete Action Plan

**Days 1-14:** Build the free AI Visibility Checker (Phase 0). Manually audit 20 target agencies/brands. Start posting build-in-public content on LinkedIn (3x/week minimum).

**Days 15-30:** Convert 5-10 of those manual audits into paying pilot customers (founder-led sales, manual onboarding is fine). Publish your first "state of AI visibility for Indian [category] brands" data report.

**Days 31-60:** Build the MVP dashboard (Part 1-5 specs) around what those pilot customers actually asked for. Approach 10-15 agencies directly with a white-label pilot offer.

**Days 61-90:** Launch self-serve signup with ₹ pricing. Push the free tool harder for lead gen. Publish 2nd data report. Target: 20-30 paying customers, 2-3 agency partners actively reselling.

---

## 6. Final Honest Note

You don't need to beat Searchable, Profound, and Scrunch at their own game on day one — you need to become the **obvious, trusted choice for Indian brands and agencies** first, the same way Searchable became obvious for a segment of Western marketers who already trusted Chris Donnelly. Win that fully, prove real revenue, then expand internationally into other underserved-language markets using the same playbook. That's a realistic, fundable, ownable path — "beat everyone everywhere immediately" is not.
